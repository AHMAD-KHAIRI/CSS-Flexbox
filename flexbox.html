Let's Play with Flexbox

The flex property sets how a flex item will grow or shrink to fit the space available in its flex container.
The flex-direction sets how flex items are placed in the flex container defining the main axis and the direction (normal or reversed).
The default MAIN-AXIS for a flex container is from left to right (flex-direction: row).
The default CROSS-AXIS is from top to bottom (flex-direction: column).
The direction can be changed in the case of row-reverse or column-reverse.
The justify-content defines how the browser distributes space between and around content items along the MAIN-AXIS of a flex container, and the inline axis of a grid container.
The flex-wrap sets whether flex items are forced onto one line or can wrap onto multiple lines.
The align-items controls the alignment of items on the CROSS-AXIS.
The align-content sets the distribution of space between and around content items along a flexbox's CROSS-AXIS or a grid's block axis.
The flex-basis defines the initial size of an element before additional space is distributed.
Flex-grow controls the amount of available space an element should take up. Accepts a unit-less number value.
Flex-shrink: if items are larger than the container, they shrink according to flex-shrink.
